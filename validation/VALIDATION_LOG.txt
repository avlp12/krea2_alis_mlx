Krea-2-Turbo · Alis MLX — quantization validation (validation/validate_quant.py)
Per-step velocity cosine vs full-precision bf16 on a FIXED trajectory (isolates
quantization error from benign ODE divergence). 12 prompts × 8 steps = 96 samples
per build. Generated on an Apple M3 Ultra. Reference (bf16) is itself cos=1.000000
vs the PyTorch reference (see README verification table).

[bf16] size=25.6GB

[8bit-bulk ] size=14.2GB  qlinears=224  fwd= 5973ms  vel_cos vs bf16: mean=0.99994 min=0.99959  (n=96)
[4bit-bulk ] size= 8.2GB  qlinears=224  fwd= 5949ms  vel_cos vs bf16: mean=0.99760 min=0.98666  (n=96)
[mixed-4/8 ] size= 9.8GB  qlinears=224  fwd= 5953ms  vel_cos vs bf16: mean=0.99824 min=0.98710  (n=96)

Notes:
- vel_cos mean/min are over the 96 (prompt,step) samples (12 diverse prompts:
  animal/snow, urban/neon, portrait, landscape, food, sci-fi, painterly, bright
  animal, dramatic figure, interior, macro, dense scene).
- fwd = mean per-step transformer latency at 1024x1024 (3 timed calls).
- latency is ~equal across bit-widths => quantization gives no speedup here
  (generation is attention-bound; attention is not quantized).
- An earlier 3-prompt (n=24) screen reported slightly higher figures (8-bit
  0.99996/0.99988, mixed 0.99849/0.99229, 4-bit 0.99788/0.98852); the 12-prompt
  set is more representative, especially for the worst-case min.
